Nothing OS vs. Other Budget Android Skins – Background App Behavior

Here's the critical context: most background app killing on Android doesn't come from the Android OS itself — it comes from the manufacturer's skin layered on top. Samsung's One UI, Xiaomi's MIUI, and Realme UI all add aggressive battery optimization layers that Google doesn't require and that most users never configure correctly.

Nothing OS has historically been the most restrained OEM skin in the budget space. It adds minimal customization on top of Android, which means fewer layers of background process interference. But "minimal" doesn't mean "zero."

✅
What Nothing Does Right
No bloatware, no aggressive background kill timers by default, near-stock app permission model. Battery optimization is conservative, not trigger-happy.
⚠️
What Still Needs Attention
8GB RAM on Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 can get tight with 8+ apps open. Heavy multitasking sessions may still trigger OS-level kills.
🔋
Battery Optimization Default
Nothing OS defaults to "Optimized" for most apps, not "Restricted." This is already better than MIUI and Realme UI out of the box.
📲
Notification Reliability
Our dontkillmyapp.com score for Nothing Phone 2 was 8/10 — among the best for budget Android. The 4b is expected to match or improve this.

How to Optimize Nothing Phone 4b for Zero Background App Killing

📋 Optimization Checklist – Nothing Phone 4b

1. Settings → Battery → Battery Optimization → set WhatsApp, Gmail, Telegram, your alarm app, and music apps to "Don't Optimize."

2. Settings → Apps → [App] → Battery → select "Unrestricted" for mission-critical apps.

3. Disable "Adaptive Battery" if you notice issues: Settings → Battery → Adaptive Battery → Off.

4. Keep background app limit at system default — don't install RAM booster apps, they make the problem worse.

5. After July 7 launch, we'll post a device-specific guide with Nothing OS 4.0 screenshots.

Should You Buy the Nothing Phone 4b?

If you're in the budget Android market and background app reliability matters to you — and it should — the Nothing Phone 4b is the safest choice at this price tier. You get near-stock software behavior, a 5,400mAh battery that won't need aggressive background killing to last the day, and Nothing's track record of clean, minimal OEM intervention.

The Snapdragon 6 Gen 4 with 8GB RAM will handle everyday multitasking without issue. Where you'll notice limits is in sustained heavy multitasking — having 10+ apps open simultaneously may eventually trigger OS-level kills. But for normal usage — messaging, social, music, navigation — the Phone 4b should deliver near-Pixel-level reliability at a significantly lower price.
